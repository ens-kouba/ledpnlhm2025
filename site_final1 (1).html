Laboratory

The Laboratory of Nonlinear Partial Differential Equations (PDEs) and the History of Mathematics was established in 2000 with four research groups, under the direction of Professor Youcef Atik (may Allah have mercy on him).

In 2008, Professor Abdelhafid Mekran assumed the directorship of the laboratory until 2023, after which he was succeeded by Professor Choutri Abdelaziz, who still serves as its head to this day.

The structure of the laboratory was later expanded to include six research teams:

  • Modeling of heat and mass transfer problems in porous media.
  • Study of nonlinear PDEs: equations, systems, and elliptic and parabolic inequalities.
  • Nonlinear convex analysis: relaxation and minimization.
  • Study of certain elliptic and parabolic equations in non-regular domains.
  • Mathematical and numerical analysis of guided mode computation problems in optical fibers: direct and inverse problems, image processing.
  • Analytic number theory and arithmetic functions.

The laboratory has 60 researchers, the majority of whom belong to ENS-Kouba. The others are associate researchers, affiliated with various national universities, having been trained within our laboratory.

Since its creation, the laboratory has organized about ten international colloquia and several scientific schools, in partnership with national and European laboratories.

It has also trained more than a hundred masterโs and doctoral graduates. Some of the research has focused on porous media, in collaboration with Sonatrach, while others were conducted under joint supervision with Sorbonne University (formerly Paris), the University of Pau and the Adour Region, as well as the University of Cartagena in Spain.

Four laboratories have emerged from our structure: two at ENS-Kouba, one at the University of Algiers, and another, recently established, at the University of Mรฉdรฉa.
